CENTRAL LIMIT THEOREM (CLT) – COMPLETE GUIDE

1. Definition:
The Central Limit Theorem states that the distribution of the sample mean (or sum) of a sufficiently large number of independent, identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variables approaches a normal distribution (Gaussian), regardless of the original distribution of the population. 

Mathematically:
If X1, X2, ..., Xn are i.i.d. random variables with mean μ and standard deviation σ, then the sampling distribution of the sample mean:
    X̄ = (X1 + X2 + ... + Xn)/n
approaches:
    X̄ ~ N(μ, σ²/n) 
as n → ∞ (large sample size).

2. Key Assumptions:
- Random Sampling: Samples must be randomly selected.
- Independence: Each sample must be independent of the others.
- Identically Distributed: All samples should come from the same population.
- Sample Size: Generally, n ≥ 30 is considered sufficient for the CLT to hold (can vary based on original distribution).

3. Importance / Why CLT is used:
- Allows approximation of sample means with normal distribution.
- Enables construction of confidence intervals and hypothesis testing even if the population is not normally distributed.
- Fundamental in inferential statistics and many statistical models.

4. Formulas:
- Sample Mean:
    X̄ = (Σ Xi) / n
- Standard Error of the Mean (SEM):
    SE = σ / √n
- Sampling Distribution of Mean:
    X̄ ~ N(μ, σ²/n)

5. Python Code Example:
# Simulating Central Limit Theorem in Python
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Population: Uniform distribution
population = np.random.uniform(low=0, high=100, size=10000)

sample_means = []
sample_size = 50
num_samples = 1000

for _ in range(num_samples):
    sample = np.random.choice(population, sample_size)
    sample_means.append(np.mean(sample))

# Plotting the sampling distribution of sample means
plt.hist(sample_means, bins=30, edgecolor='black')
plt.title("Sampling Distribution of Sample Means (CLT)")
plt.xlabel("Sample Mean")
plt.ylabel("Frequency")
plt.show()

# Mean and Standard Deviation of Sampling Distribution
print("Mean of sample means:", np.mean(sample_means))
print("Standard Error:", np.std(sample_means))

6. Notes:
- Even if the population is not normally distributed, the CLT ensures that the sampling distribution of the mean will be approximately normal for a large sample size.
- This theorem justifies using normal probability theory in inferential statistics.
